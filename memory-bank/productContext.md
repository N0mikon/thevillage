# Product Context: The Village

## Why This Project Exists

### Problem Statement
The incremental/idle game genre has many successful titles, but there's an opportunity to create a village-themed management game that focuses specifically on peasant settlement development. There's room for a more focused, village-centric experience.

### Target Audience
- **Primary**: Fans of incremental/idle games who enjoy resource management
- **Secondary**: Players who enjoy city/village building games
- **Tertiary**: Casual gamers looking for engaging browser-based games

### Unique Value Proposition
- **Village Focus**: Unlike many incremental games that focus on abstract concepts, this game centers on tangible village management
- **Peasant Workforce**: The core mechanic revolves around managing and growing a peasant population
- **Medieval Theme**: Provides a familiar, engaging setting that's easy to understand
- **Accessible Complexity**: Simple to start but deep enough for long-term engagement

## Problems It Solves

### For Players
1. **Engagement**: Provides a satisfying progression loop with clear goals
2. **Accessibility**: Easy to understand medieval village theme
3. **Depth**: Multiple interconnected systems create strategic decisions
4. **Convenience**: Browser-based, no installation required
5. **Persistence**: Save system allows for long-term play sessions

### For the Genre
1. **Variety**: Adds a village-themed option to the incremental game space
2. **Innovation**: Focuses on population management rather than abstract numbers
3. **Accessibility**: Simpler theme than many existing incremental games

## How It Should Work

### Core Gameplay Loop
1. **Gather Resources**: Start by manually collecting basic resources
2. **Recruit Peasants**: Use resources to grow your population
3. **Assign Jobs**: Give peasants specific roles for specialized production
4. **Build Infrastructure**: Construct buildings to automate and enhance production
5. **Research Upgrades**: Spend knowledge to unlock improvements
6. **Explore Territory**: Expand your village's reach for new opportunities
7. **Optimize**: Balance different systems for maximum efficiency

### User Experience Goals

#### Early Game (First 30 minutes)
- **Immediate Engagement**: Clear, simple actions with visible results
- **Learning Curve**: Gradual introduction of new systems
- **Sense of Progress**: Frequent unlocks and improvements
- **Tutorial Integration**: Natural learning through gameplay

#### Mid Game (30 minutes to 2 hours)
- **Strategic Depth**: Multiple viable approaches to optimization
- **Automation**: Transition from manual to automated gameplay
- **Exploration**: New areas and challenges to discover
- **Customization**: Player choice in development path

#### Late Game (2+ hours)
- **Long-term Goals**: Prestige-like mechanics or major milestones
- **Complexity**: Multiple interconnected systems requiring optimization
- **Discovery**: Hidden mechanics and advanced strategies
- **Community**: Sharing strategies and achievements

## Success Metrics

### Player Engagement
- **Session Length**: Average play session duration
- **Return Rate**: How often players come back to the game
- **Progression Rate**: How quickly players advance through content
- **Completion Rate**: Percentage of players who reach end-game content

### Technical Performance
- **Load Time**: Game initialization speed
- **Responsiveness**: UI interaction lag
- **Stability**: Crash and error rates
- **Save Reliability**: Data persistence success rate

### Content Effectiveness
- **System Usage**: Which game systems are most/least used
- **Balance**: Resource and progression balance
- **Clarity**: How well players understand the game mechanics
- **Satisfaction**: Player feedback and enjoyment levels
